Intro to Self-Driving Cars

CURC and ADI Academy will collaborate on teaching an Intro to Self-Driving Cars course to the Columbia community using the NVIDIA Jetson TX1 Robotics Teaching Kit. The course content and the robotics kit used in this workshop series were designed by NVIDIA. It is composed of ten complete code modules that guide students through building the robot, and programming various autonomous functionality (computer vision, behavioral cloning, localization filters, PID control, and SLAM) using C++ and ROS. NVIDIA’s content will be supplemented with free content from Udacity’s AI for Robotics course and Stanford’s CS231n course. Students in this course will learn foundational knowledge useful for understanding and pursuing further opportunities in autonomous robots/self-driving cars.

Mech Warfare (2016 - 2017)

Project Lead and Robot Programming: Shikhar Bakhda; (SEAS 2020)
Chassis design and Fabrication Lead: Albert Tai (SEAS 2020)
Gun Design Lead: Nick Greene (Computer Engineering 2019)
Team: Ben Israeli (Applied Physics 2017), David Lalo (CC 2020), Rayal Raj-Prasad

The Mech Warfare team builds upon the 2014-2015 team's progress, to compete in the Mech Warfare challenge at RoboGames 2017. The competition places small biped and quadrupedal “mechs” with mounted airsoft pellet guns in simulated battle scenarios. The matches are scored using target panels and are arranged in a tournament-style event. Competitors are required to pilot the mechs remotely via video feed, giving controlling the robot the feeling of playing a first-person shooter.

This year's robot is propelled on 4 legs, each with 3 degrees of freedom. Each joint will be actuated by a separate AX-12A digital servo. Control will be provided by a microcontroller running the basic gait engine and quadruped design suite PyPose.

Autonomous Drone (2016 - 2017)

Project Leads: Rachel Yang (CS 2018) and Vinay Mehta (Computer Engineering 2018)

Fall 2016

The Autonomous Drone team completed TUM's edX course Autonomous Navigation for Flying Robots, and studied and modified the source code of TUM's ARDrone and Vision4UAV's cvg_ardrone2_ibvs to understand how autonomous quadcopter systems work.

Spring 2017

The Autonomous Drone Team worked on implementing a ROS autonomous navigation system on the Intel Aero Ready-to-Fly Drone platform, using knowledge of the two systems studied in Fall 2016. GitHub: columbia-robotics-drone

Mech Warfare (2014 - 2015)

In 2014-2015, led by Ben Israeli (SEAS 2017), several members built a remotely piloted walker equipped with a low power BB gun, FPV camera, and scoring system.
